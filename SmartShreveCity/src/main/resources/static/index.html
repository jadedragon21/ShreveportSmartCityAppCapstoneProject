WELCOME TO SHREVEPORT

Restaurants

Louisiana’s own ethnic Creole and Cajun cuisines are a great way to begin culinary sampling, and diners can also enjoy Tex-Mex barbecue style cooking, classic Mexican, French, Italian, Chinese, Japanese, Thai, Greek, Polynesian, Indian food, and the ever popular seafood and steak combination.

Festivals

There are many festivals in Shreveport and the neighboring areas.During four days over each Memorial Day weekend for the last 25 years, the Mudbug Madness Festival has been celebrated in Shreveport’s Festival Plaza. Featuring both Creole and Cajun foods, Mudbug Madness includes a huge public seafood boil that residents call the granddaddy of them all. A more recent culinary tradition called the Annual Gentlemen's Cooking Classic is sponsored by the Southern University at Shreveport Foundation.

Entertainment

Shreveport’s museums and art galleries provide an ever changing variety of exhibits for people to enjoy while the cultural festivals that occur each month provide another enjoyable opportunity for family entertainment. And for those who have fun shopping, the Louisiana Boardwalk in Bossier City is the largest combination shopping, dining and entertainment center in Louisiana.
